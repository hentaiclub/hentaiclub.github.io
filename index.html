Vernacular Culture and its Aesthetics


This is a collaborative project done in conjunction with Adeline Ang as part of our final year project. We were exploring the aesthetics of Vernacular design. Instead of rejecting what Modernist designers would classify as 'bad design', we talked about how these 'bad designs' can be a positive influence to designers of today. Thus, recreating the 'ugly' into something beautiful.

Panic! Zine


Panic Zine tells the story about the origin of the word 'Panic'. The word derives from the Greek God named 'Pan'. Pan had a loud, terrifying voice that scared his enemies away during a war. To represent the impact of Pan, this zine is intended to defy and destruct the design rules, letting loose of its structure which allows a playful manipulation of the typography and layout. It also consists bursts of loud colors to convey the blaring sound of Pan. The use of marble-textured paper is symbolic to the ancient mythology.


Dimensions: 175mm X 240mm

[Untitled Archive]


Done as a gift for my family, [Untitled Archive] features a series of photographs I took during a family trip to Taiwan. The intention was to preserve the memories, and to make the ambience of trip tangible. This book takes on a melancholic and poetic direction due to the condition of the weather during the trip. It was rainy and foggy, and inevitably leaving a pinch of gloominess within us.

To translate that into this book, the cover remains in black and white, and atop, sits a smaller booklet which includes a collection of Chinese and English poems. To mimic the foggy atomosphere, I have used a translucent paper stock for the chapter openers. That allows the contents of the page beneath to be visible. As the reader flips the page, the contents gradually disppears. Thus, allowing the reader to experience that idea of a fog.


Dimensions: 175mm X 240mm

Typology: A Classification of Typefaces


The contemporary design of the book displays a combination of 'structure' and 'playfulness'. The sophisticated placement of elements builds the structure. While the 'playfulness' is found within the contents where design-related riddles are used to showcase the typefaces. This tone of humor helps to entice the reader and keep them engaged. The royal blue color also sits in between the spectrum of dull to vibrant, thus balancing that equilibrium. Although the book is set in one main color, slight touches of grey were added to the font diagrams for a subtle hierarchy.

The specimen booklet features the chosen typeface 'Albertus' which was designed back in 1940. The booklet uses a grey paper stock not only to maintain the consistency of the blue/grey harmony in the book, but its muskiness also enhances the impression of an older typeface. Instead of having two separate publications, the books have been bound together where the layering effect and exposed binding method deviates from the standard construction of a book. Thus, reenacting the intended playfulness.


Dimensions: 200mm X 290mm

STIR


STIR is a Design Conference which explores the correlation between graphic design and psychology. The task was to design a 'provaction' for it. Using the brain and a link cursor as semiotics, it implements the effect that design has on the human mind.

The brochure maintains the same concept. However, it features different semiotics where arrow cursors are seen poking at the human gut instead. This digital distortion is also applied to the typography of the conference title and names of speakers. Thus, building a stronger and more distinctively recognizable identity for the conference.


Dimensions: 594mm x 841mm (Posters),
148mm x 210mm (Brochure)
